Home in Putrajaya, Kuala Lumpur: welcome!

Putrajaya, Malaysia's administrative capital, lies 25 kilometers south of Kuala Lumpur. It was developed to ease congestion in Kuala Lumpur, featuring modern architecture and green spaces. Putrajaya serves as the federal government's administrative hub, while Kuala Lumpur thrives as the commercial and cultural center, known for its vibrancy and diversity.

Seri Gemilang Bridge

The Seri Gemilang Bridge is an iconic structure in Putrajaya, Malaysia. It's known for its elegant design and serves as a landmark in the city. The bridge spans across the Putrajaya Lake, connecting Precinct 4 to Precinct 3. With its graceful arches and decorative fearures, the Seri Gemilang Bridge adds to the beauty of Putrajaya's landscape and is often admired by visitors for its aestheti appeal.

Masjid Putra

Masjid Putra is a prominent landmark in Putrajaya. Known for its striking pink-domed roof and elegant Islamic architecturem, it accommodates thousands of worshippers. Visitors can admire its beauty, serene ambiance, and picturesque surroundings, including Putrajaya Lake. Open to tourists, the mosque offers guided tours and insights into Islamic culture and architecture.
